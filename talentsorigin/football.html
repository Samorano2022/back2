History of Nigerian Football
As the most populous country in Africa and seventh in the world, the passion for sports in Nigeria is unwavering. Several decades after its British colonial independence, it has quickly grown to become a home to some of the greatest and richest athletes and sporting events on the continent. Among many, the most widely celebrated sport in the country is football. Its a love affair that has blossomed for several years. It serves as a religion where half of the population, about 100miIIion Nigerians, are active football fans and consumers. That said, before the sport turned into this grand fervent culture and massive money industry, it was initially a symbol of freedom and nationalism in the second half of the 20th century.
Club Competition
The grandeur and gravity of Nigerian Football follow a series of connected leagues. It pyramid is made up of three levels, the top flight is Nigeria Premier League [NPFL], is the elite league of Nigeria Football Federation [NFF]. embracing the 20 most competitive clubs sides in the country, where four of the 20 top of the line clubs are relegated to the second tier called the Nigerian National League [NNL], then six of its 32 clubs are demoted in level three named Nigeria Nationwide league [NNWL] whereas the other four are promoted to the first level.

B - How Does the Show Works
A contestant must answer 10 questions correctly in a row to win the jackpot. A contestant may quit at anytime and keep his earnings. For each question the contestant is shown four possible answers in advance before deciding whether to play or not. A contestant will be giving two life lines i.e phone a friend or ask the audience if the question is difficult to answer.
